# **Cells of the Nervous System**
## **1. Introduction to Nervous System Cells**
- The brain consists of two main types of cells:
  - **Neurons (~86 billion)** → Information processing.
  - **Glial cells (~85 billion)** → Support and protection (Greek: "glue").
---
## **2. Counting Nerve Cells ("Brain Soup")**
### **Distribution of Neurons and Glial Cells**
- **Cerebral Cortex (82% of brain mass)**:
  - **~16 billion neurons** (19% of all brain neurons).
  - **~61 billion glial cells**.
- **Cerebellum (10% of brain mass)**:
  - **~69 billion neurons** (80% of all brain neurons).
  - **~16 billion glial cells**.
- **Rest of the Brain (8% of brain mass)**:
  - **~1 billion neurons** (1% of all brain neurons).
  - **~8 billion glial cells**.

- [[@Herculano-Houzel_2014]]

## **3. Neurons: Basic Structure and Function**
### **3.1. Three Main Parts of a Neuron**
| **Part**        | **Function** |
|---------------|-----------------|
| **Dendrites** (many) | Collect **input** from other neurons. |
| **Cell body (soma)** | Integrates information. |
| **Axon** (one) | Sends **output** to other neurons. |
**Flow of Information in a Neuron**:
1. **Input** → **Dendrites**.
2. **Integration** → **Cell Body**.
3. **Output** → **Axon**.
### **3.2. Three Major Types of Neurons**
| **Type**          | **Function** |
|-----------------|---------------|
| **Sensory Neurons (Afferent, Input)** | Carry information **into** the CNS. |
| **Interneurons (Association Neurons)** | Connect neurons within the CNS. |
| **Motor Neurons (Efferent, Output)** | Carry information **out** to muscles and glands. |
**Exceptions to Simplified Categories**:
- Some sensory neurons have **short dendrites** (e.g., **bipolar neurons in the retina**).
- Some interneurons have **long axons** (e.g., **pyramidal cells in the cortex**).

# **4. Glial Cells: Support Cells**
### **4.1. Functions of Glial Cells**
- Provide **physical support**.
- Deliver **nutrients**.
- **Increase conduction** speed of nerve impulses.
- **Protect neurons** from damage and infection.
**Glial cells DO NOT transmit electrical signals** like neurons.
### **4.2. Five Major Types of Glial Cells**
| **Type**          | **Function** |
|-----------------|--------------|
| **Ependymal Cells** | Produce cerebrospinal fluid (CSF). |
| **Astrocytes** | Structural support, nutrient transport, blood-brain barrier. |
| **Microglia** | Immune defense, remove dead neurons. |
| **Oligodendrocytes (CNS)** | Myelinate axons in the **CNS**. |
| **Schwann Cells (PNS)** | Myelinate axons in the **PNS**, aid repair. |

---
# **5. Specialized Glial Cells**
### **5.1. Ependymal Cells**
- Found in **ventricles** of the brain.
- Produce **cerebrospinal fluid (CSF)** → cushions the brain.
### **5.2. Astrocytes**
- **Scaffold cells** → Provide structural support.
- **Transport nutrients** between neurons and blood vessels.
- **Form the Blood-Brain Barrier (BBB)** → Prevents toxins from entering the brain.
- **Aid in brain repair** (but may cause **scarring**).
### **5.3. Microglia (Brain's Immune System)**
- Originate **from the immune system** (unlike other glial cells).
- Detect and remove **damaged cells, plaques (Alzheimer's), and infections**.
- Provide **growth factors** to aid in repair.
### **5.4. Oligodendrocytes & Schwann Cells**
| **Cell Type** | **Function** |
|-------------|-------------------|
| **Oligodendrocytes (CNS)** | Myelinate multiple neurons in the **brain and spinal cord**. |
| **Schwann Cells (PNS)** | Myelinate **individual axons** in **peripheral nerves**. |
**Key Difference in Repair Ability**:
- **Schwann cells** → Aid in **repair** → PNS can regenerate.
- **Oligodendrocytes** → **Do not support repair** → CNS damage is permanent (e.g., spinal cord injury).

# **6. Studying Brain Cells (Histology)**
### **Techniques Used**
- **Traditional Staining** → Works only in **dead tissue** (ex vivo).
- **Modern Imaging (in vivo)**:
  - **Clarity** → Makes tissue transparent.
  - **Brainbow** → Transgenic technique, color-codes neurons.
  - **Diffusion Tensor Imaging (DTI)** → Tracks white matter pathways.

# **7. Internal Structure of a Neuron**
| **Organelle** | **Function** |
|--------------|-------------|
| **Cell Membrane** | Encloses and protects the neuron. |
| **Nucleus** | Contains DNA (genetic information). |
| **Endoplasmic Reticulum (ER)** | **Synthesizes proteins**. |
| **Golgi Bodies** | **Package and transport proteins**. |
| **Microtubules** | **Transport proteins and chemicals**. |
| **Mitochondria** | **Generate energy (ATP)** for the neuron. |
| **Lysosomes** | Remove waste materials. |

# **8. Cell Membrane & Transport**
### **8.1. Structure**
- **Phospholipid bilayer**:
  - **Hydrophilic heads** (water-attracting).
  - **Hydrophobic tails** (water-repelling).
### **8.2. Membrane Proteins**
| **Type** | **Function** |
|---------|-------------|
| **Channel** | Passive transport of specific ions. |
| **Gate** | Opens or closes based on signals. |
| **Pump** | **Active transport** (e.g., sodium/potassium pump). |

# **9. Genetics and Protein Synthesis**
### **9.1. Chromosomes and DNA**
- **23 pairs of chromosomes** (one from each parent).
- **Genes** = Segments of **DNA** that code for proteins.
- **Alleles** = Different versions of a gene.
  - **Homozygous** = Identical alleles.
  - **Heterozygous** = Different alleles.
**Genetic Mutations**:
- **Neutral** (no effect).
- **Beneficial** (e.g., increased bone density).
- **Harmful** (e.g., sickle cell disease, cystic fibrosis).
### **9.2. Protein Synthesis**
1. **DNA transcription** → mRNA is made.
2. **mRNA translation** → Ribosomes use mRNA to assemble amino acids.
3. **Protein folding** → Creates functional proteins.
**Protein synthesis is the basis of neural function and behavior**.
